\section{Quantitative Results}

\paragraph{Latency.}
Tracking-loop latency (capture-to-pose) was measured with the Python application using \texttt{--log-latency} under the chosen configuration (backend, device, resolution). Results are reported as mean and 95th percentile (p95) in milliseconds, with sample size. These values are compared to the 63\,ms (cybersickness) and 125\,ms (body ownership) thresholds from the literature; the aim is to remain well below 63\,ms so that total motion-to-photon (including rendering) can stay within a comfortable range.

\paragraph{Engagement and other measures.}
Engagement scores and any other quantitative measures defined in the methodology (e.g.\ time-on-task, questionnaire scores) are reported here. The questionnaire or scale used for engagement (e.g.\ User Engagement Scale or a custom Likert scale) is to be specified in the Supplementary Material (Appendix~\ref{app:supp}) once the pilot is finalised. Tables or figures may be added as the pilot data become available.
