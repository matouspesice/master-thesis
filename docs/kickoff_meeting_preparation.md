## 1. Thesis Topic

> **Development of a Low-Latency ML-Based Motion Tracking System for Gamified Knee Rehabilitation in Virtual Reality**

---

## 2. Problem Statement / Motivation

**What problem are you trying to solve?**
- Current rehabilitation setups suffer from **high latency (~200ms)**, causing "laggy" avatar behavior that breaks immersion and hinders therapy effectiveness
- Traditional knee exercises **lack engagement**, leading to poor patient compliance
- Need to address the technical bottleneck of latency while introducing gamification to improve therapy outcomes

**Why is this relevant/important?**
- Poor patient compliance is a major issue in rehabilitation
- Immersion in VR therapy depends heavily on responsive feedback
- Combining low-latency tracking with gamification could significantly improve rehabilitation outcomes

---

## 3. Research Questions

**Aim:**
> To create a real-time VR feedback loop where an avatar responds to skeletal tracking within **<10ms**

**Leading Question:**
> How does a low-latency, goal-oriented "Architect" game influence the stability and engagement of patients during single-leg balance exercises compared to standard visual feedback?

---

## 4. Methodology / Planned Strategy

### 4.1 Hardware/Software Pipeline
- Integration of a **standard RGB camera** with ML skeletal extraction
- Replace high-latency legacy systems with optimized ML-based tracking

### 4.2 Gamification Implementation
- Develop a VR exercise prototype centered on **proprioceptive training**
- Use a goal-oriented **"Architect" game mechanic**
- Shift patient focus from internal joint movement to external virtual objectives

### 4.3 User Evaluation
- Pilot study focusing on **"System Feel"**
- Measure user perception of responsiveness (latency)
- Compare engagement levels vs. traditional exercises
